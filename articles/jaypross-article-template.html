Foundation: Key PM Roles and Responsibilities Ripe for AI Takeover

If you have strategy, product, program, project, design, research, analyst, or analytics in your title in any form and you work on software products, then your job is likely to change, or even be eliminated by AI in the near future.

Welcome to the first of three articles on the topic of whether AI can replace Product Managers. In these three articles, I will be exploring how much of the product management role can be replaced or augmented by AI.

Foundation Part One: The Product Lifecycle and Common Redundancies

All products follow the same product lifecycle: Concept, Discovery, Launch, Growth, Maintenance, and Death. Within these phases you have different roles, skills, and titles that can own them or contribute to them.

I purposely made the list of roles vague. As mentioned in my intro to this series, most companies do not really plan their organization structure. It tends to happen organically based on strengths and weaknesses within the team. This organic approach is how you end up with responsibilities in odd places and how you end up with redundancy when the company matures.

There is a very common type of true redundancy that has emerged more recently that is fairly easy to eliminate manually and even easier to automate away with AI. This is when your Product Team operates like a Feature Team.

Feature Teams vs. Product Teams

Here are a few things that are common in companies with Feature Teams:

  • Product strategy and feature decisions are made outside of product management.
  • The engineering teams own most internal product development activities.
  • Access to customers and users is managed by a separate team.

If you find yourself working as a product manager in a company with feature teams, your role is likely redundant with other departments and ripe for being replaced with AI.

Foundation Part Two: Who Is at Risk?

The first group of people at risk are what I call information managers. Regardless of your title or department, if your job in any aspect of product management is about moving data around, you are at risk.

If any of the below are true, it is possible for AI to impact your role in the near future:

  • You have strategy, product, program, project, design, research, analyst, or analytics in your title.
  • Your company has separate departments for business strategy, product management, design, program management, and project management all touching the same product.
  • A bulk of your duties involve moving data about product between systems to keep other people up to date.

In the next article I will show you how AI can do all these things and prove that it is real. In the final article I will walk you through what I think the role of Product should be, and how those at risk can best prepare themselves to excel.
